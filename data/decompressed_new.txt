Huffman coding is a lossless data compression algorithm that assigns variable-length codes to characters based on their frequencies.
Characters that appear more frequently are assigned shorter codes, while less frequent characters get longer codes.
This approach minimizes the total number of bits needed to encode the entire message or file.
The algorithm builds a binary tree where frequent characters are closer to the root, ensuring shorter paths and codes.
